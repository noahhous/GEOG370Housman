In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

By changing the Project Coordinate Reference System in the project properties, I was able to transform the shapefile into different world projections. I also used the Indicatrix plug-in to help visualize the effects and distortions of each projection.

WGS84 Projection

This projection is one that many of us are used to seeing. It appears to distort the shape of land masses as it goes closer to the poles. Overall this is a very common and standard projection.

Aitoff Projection

This projection appears to preserve the prime meridian as a straight line. It increasingly curves out the longitudinal lines as it moves outwards from the prime meridian.

Pseudo-Mercator Projection

This projection is similar to the Mercator projection, except that it also distorts along longitudinal lines, whereas the Mercator projection only distorts along latitudinal lines. It seems as if it would be useful for visualizing the placement of individal countries or land masses.

Sphere Winkel Projection

This projection appears to preserve the straightness of the prime meridian while relaying an elipsoid shape. It appears to do this by skewing cordinates in a diagonal direction, either away from or towards the prime meridian.

World Cylindrical Equal Area Projection

This projection appears to be a simple cylindrical projection. There appears to be a great deal of both longitudinal and latitudinal distortion as it gets closer to the poles. The equal area of the Indicatrix indicators appears to show that countries and land masses preserve their comparative area no matter their location on the projection.

World Equidistant Conic

This projection appears to be a fairly standard conic projection. It distorts a great deal as it moves away from the North Pole, but it appears to preserve latitudinal distance fairly well. There is, however, a large gap in the top.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant

This appears to be a projection centered on the North Pole and projecting outward (Southward) onto the rest of the globe. There appears to be similar distortion but a more complete picture compared to the previous projection. The gap appears much smaller, although still present.

Van Der Grinten Projection

This projection is interesting because it appears to attempt to preserve very little. It preserves the general shapes of other projections (such as WSG84), but it does not preserve area. I like that it gives the general idea of a sphere though.

Cassini Projection

I have very little idea as to what this projection is attempting to portray. After some research I figured out that it makes the central meridian act as the equator. Still, I'm not sure what you could gain from using this projection.
